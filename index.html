Voyage

The mobile app that takes the hassle out of planning an itinerary, allowing travelers to efficiently find and book activities based on their specific interests.

Research

Interviews

I started the research phase of this project by interviewing five users. My objective was to gain information about the preferences, goals and frustrations users have in regards to traveling.

Sample Questions:

-How many times a year do you travel?

-What kind of electronic devices do you own and which ones do you bring while traveling?

-What is your motivation for traveling?

-What frustrations do you have in terms of planning/staying organized on a trip?

-Walk me through a typical day on vacation for you?

"On vacation I relax by the pool, enjoy food, go sight seeing, socialize with friends and do various activities."

"I typically find activities by doing research online, reading local magazines, or talking to concierge/locals."

"I start with a tentative plan, but I like to wait and see what options are available there and then I plan more."

"It's hard dealing with a group of people while traveling and being on the same page with activities."

User Persona

I would like to introduce you to the main character of this story, my user persona Liz Palmer. I chose to focus on her as the main persona moving forward in this case study.

-I wanted to create a strong persona that would illustrate a targeted group of users for Voyage.

-This allowed me to empathize with users and focus more on their goals/needs.

-Including a "Motivations" section was important to highlight why users feel the need to utilize Voyage.

-Frequently referencing Liz Palmer helped me create effective solutions for the main problem.

Define & Ideate

Liz Palmer, an avid traveler with little free time needs to easily plan/organize many different activities for her group to do whie on vacation, because everyone has different interests/hobbies.

It is time consuming and overwhelming for travelers to sort through and plan activities for trips/vacations that are aligned with their individual interests.

Journey Map

After diving deeper into my main persona Liz Palmer and thoroughly understanding her feelings/emotions, I was able to organize a journey map for her.

-I included a specific scenario to help support the steps and reasons for Liz's journey through Voyage.

-I chose to focus on Liz adding a trip and selecting an activity in Voyage since these were two main features I was planning on showcasing.

-Going through each step of Liz's journey helped me brainstorm various ways to bring Voyage to life.

-I wanted to start with Liz's main needs so that I could show how Voyage successfully addresses those needs in a fun and positive way.

Prototype

Sketches & Iterations

I started sketching out some of my ideas in order to get feedback from testing.

Notes/Feedback:

-Clear layout, easy to grasp functions, positive and linear process.

-Onboarding is too simple, need more details throughout.

-Layout of home page is awkward, need to clarify and add more details for activity sections.

-Add more personal touches for users.

After gathering notes and feedback from usability testing I began iterating my sketches to change some of the layouts, add more details and update the overall flow of the app.

I focused more on the onboarding process because I recieved the most feedback for that portion of the app. I also included ideas for a profile section that had more personal aspects to it.

Final Wireframes

My main problem statement focused on how time consuming and overwhelming it is for travlers to find activities to do. So as I began to turn my sketches into wireframes I focused on creating simple, consistent and effective designs. I felt that this was the strongest approach to take in order to successfully solve the original problem.
